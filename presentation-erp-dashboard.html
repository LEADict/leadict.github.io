Kaleidoscope - observer of beautiful forms

Wikipedia

A kaleidoscope is a cylinder with mirrors containing loose, colored objects such as beads or pebbles and bits of glass. As the viewer looks into one end, light entering the other creates a colorful pattern, due to the reflection off of the mirrors. Coined in 1817 by Scottish inventor Sir David Brewster, "kaleidoscope" is derived from the Ancient Greek kalos, "beautiful, beauty", eidos, "that which is seen: form, shape" and skopeo, "to look to, to examine", hence "observer of beautiful forms."

Get down to business

Four distinct Functional Areas around which firms are organized

  • Accounting and Finance: accounting, financial reporting, performance management, corporate governance.
  • Human Resources: employee recruitment, assignment tracking, performance reviews, etc.
  • Marketing and Sales: managing a company's interactions with customers, clients, and sales prospects.
  • Supply Chain Management: execute supply chain transactions, manage supplier relationships and control associated business processes.

Core Business Processes

  • Order-to-Cash: the processes associated with selling a product or service.
  • Procure-to-Pay: the processes associated with procuring goods from external vendors.
  • Make-to-Stock/Make-to-Order: the processes associated with producing goods to place in stock, or to fill a specific order.
