What We Looked For?

Our goal is to find a modern solution for eForm automation with Digital Signatures to meet these six criteria.

  • Open Technology: We want Open Standards and Open Source Software.
  • Web-Based Form: A pure web-based fillable form living in browser without plugins.
  • Digital Signature: Not based on classic Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) and hardware tokens.
  • Workflow System: Simple Workflow automation.
  • Document Storage: Archive form submition to PDF/A.
  • Mobile: iOS, Android and Windows Phone 7.

Main problems

Let us begin by considering the five main problems of ECM systems:

  • Price: Traditional ECM providers offer overpriced, overly complex systems. True costs of the full ECM Suite is even beyond some enterprises' budget.
  • Post PC era: The future of the IT infrastructure is with Web-based, flexible systems in sharp contrast with bulky, stale enterprise technology.
  • Digital Signatures: Classic PKI based digital signatures are costly and inpractical to use and they are not widespread.
  • Heavy-Weight: Big, bulky enterprise systems, with their perpetual license agreements and maintenance contracts are the dinosaurs of the enterprise IT world.
  • Proprietary software risk: On 8 November 2011, Adobe Systems announced that it has restructured its business to focus on digital media and digital marketing and will lay off 750 employees. During a meeting with financial analysts, Adobe CTO Kevin Lynch said: "Adobe will ramp down its investment and its focus on LiveCycle...".

Heading for new horizons.

Proposed Solution

The design proposed in this presentation is based on these novel ideas.

  1. XHTML5 Forms

    - as a lightweight replacement for powerfull, complex and not widespread adopted xForms. An inline Scalable Vector Graphics (SVG) act as a form boilerplate and is merged with html5 form elements. Form fields can be prepopulated with JSON data and form data is submitted in JSON format. XHTML5 Forms run on any modern browser incuding tablets and mobile phones.

  2. Mozilla's BrowserID Signature

    - for signing JSON-formatted objects (JOSE specifications). BrowserID authentication system can prove that form data is signed with email address owner. Ownership identity is more legal than technical concept and can be prove through additional legal process.

  3. WSO2 Lean Enterprise Middleware Platform

    - includes a full suite of products including Business Process Server and Data Services Server for forms automation and forms data archiving. WSO2 Suite can provide complete middleware platform that can be run on-premise, in the private cloud, and is also available as a Service (PaaS).
